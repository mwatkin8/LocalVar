LocalVar

Relation to existing services

There are a growing number of genetic variant databases, most of which include insightful annotations:

  • ClinVar - "aggregates information about genomic variation and its relationship to human health."
  • ClinGen - "a central resource that defines the clinical relevance of genes and variants for use in precision medicine and research."
  • dbSNP - "contains human single nucleotide variations, microsatellites, and small-scale insertions and deletions along with publication, population frequency, molecular consequence, and genomic and RefSeq mapping information for both common variations and clinical mutations."
  • gnomAD - "aggregates and harmonizes both exome and genome sequencing data from a wide variety of large-scale sequencing projects and makes summary data available for the wider scientific community."
  • dbVar - "NCBI's database of human genomic Structural Variation — large variants >50 bp including insertions, deletions, duplications, inversions, mobile elements, translocations, and complex variants."
  • CIViC - "open access, open source, community-driven web resource for Clinical Interpretation of Variants in Cancer."
  • COSMIC - "the Catalogue Of Somatic Mutations In Cancer, is the world's largest and most comprehensive resource for exploring the impact of somatic mutations in human cancer."
In response to this rising volume of variant data, REST-based tools have also been created to map between genome versions and harmonize identifiers across databases:
  • ClinGen Allele Registry - "provides and maintains identifiers for genetic variants."
  • MyVariant.info - "simple-to-use REST web services to query/retrieve variant annotation data, aggregated from many popular data resources."
However, each of these services are designed for query-based knowledge retrieval. In practice, most variant data is stored in local collections that require custom tooling to track provenance, record edit history, update HGVS expressions, and update interpretation information.

At a Glance...

LocalVar allows you to conveniently manage your local variant collection with the following features:

  1. Auto-complete search bar to pre-empt duplicates
  2. Single or bulk new data entry
  3. Auto-detection of duplicate entries
  4. Auto-detection and merge suggestions for HGVS synonyms
  5. Asynchronous suggestion of HGVS or Interpretation updates
  6. History tracking of additions, merges, updates, or other edits
  7. Easy export of the collection (.csv), history (.json), or synonym bins (.json)
